Partnership Agreement

The principles that govern us.

The following principles will be formalised in a Shareholders Agreement, drafted and executed by a qualified advocate upon company registration.

Legal Instrument

A Shareholders Agreement will be drafted and signed before any shares are transferred. This agreement governs all aspects of the partnership — from voting rights to exit provisions — and is binding on both parties.

  1. Simple Majority (51%+): Day-to-day operational matters, hiring of staff below senior level, approval of standard contracts, and routine financial transactions.
  2. Supermajority (75%+): Admission of new shareholders, amendment of Articles of Association, disposal of major assets, and any borrowing above KES 1,000,000.
  3. Profit Distribution: Net profits distributed 60% to Edwin William Otieno and 40% to the business partner — after all company obligations, tax liabilities, and agreed reinvestments are met.
  4. Share Transfer Restrictions: Neither partner may transfer, sell, pledge, or dispose of shares without the prior written consent of the other partner. A right of first refusal applies to any proposed transfer.
  5. Deadlock Resolution: In the event of a material business deadlock, the matter is referred to a mutually agreed independent mediator before any legal proceedings are initiated.
  6. Executive Removal: A partner may be removed from executive duties (not shareholding) by a 75% resolution in cases of proven gross misconduct, sustained incapacity, or material breach of the Shareholders Agreement.
  7. Quarterly Reviews: Formal quarterly partners' meetings to review financial performance, operations, and strategic direction — minutes recorded and retained.
