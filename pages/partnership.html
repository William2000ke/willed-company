Partnership Agreement

The principles that govern us.

These principles will be formalised in a Shareholders Agreement drafted by a qualified advocate upon company registration.

  1. Simple Majority (51%+): Day-to-day operational matters, hiring below senior level, standard contracts, and routine financial transactions.
  2. Supermajority (75%+): Admission of new shareholders, amendment of Articles of Association, disposal of major assets, and borrowing above KES 1,000,000.
  3. Profit Distribution: Net profits distributed 60% to Edwin William Otieno and 40% to the business partner — after all company obligations, tax liabilities, and agreed reinvestments are met.
  4. Share Transfer Restrictions: Neither partner may transfer, sell, or pledge shares without prior written consent of the other partner. A right of first refusal applies.
  5. Deadlock Resolution: In the event of a material business deadlock, the matter is referred to a mutually agreed independent mediator before any legal proceedings.
  6. Executive Removal: A partner may be removed from executive duties (not shareholding) by a 75% resolution in cases of gross misconduct or sustained incapacity.
  7. Quarterly Reviews: Formal quarterly partners' meetings to review performance, finances, and strategy — minutes recorded and retained.
